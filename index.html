Film Cameras

The 35 mm format, or simply 35 mm, is the common name for the 36x24 mm film format or image sensor format used in photography. It has an aspect ratio of 3:2, and a diagonal measurement of approximately 43 mm.

Medium Format

The size of the imaging sensor comes into play in the final quality of the photo. I'd rather have a 20 megapixel medium format camera than a 30 megapixel 35mm based DSLR because the physical sensor is much larger. The larger sensor the higher the quality.

Digital Camera

A digital camera that encodes digital images and videos digitally and stores them for later reproduction. Most cameras sold today are digital, and digital cameras are incorporated into many devices ranging from PDAs and mobile phones.

Mirrorless Camera

A mirrorless interchangeable lens camera is a digital camera with an interchangeable lens. A mirrorless camera uses an image sensor to provide an image to the electronic viewfinder. It is called mirrorless since it does not have a movable mirror in the optical path.
